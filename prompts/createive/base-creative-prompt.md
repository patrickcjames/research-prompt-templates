* **Reference Assets:** [Links or descriptions of existing brand assets to match or evolve.]

## Output Structure
Use ATX-style Markdown headers (`#`, `##`).

The final output should be structured as:

* `## Creative Summary`  
  [2–4 sentences summarizing the asset concept, purpose, and approach.]

* `### Core Concept`  
  [Describe the main creative idea and how it delivers on the objective.]

* `### Narrative & Messaging`  
  - **Key Message:** [Clear statement.]  
  - **Supporting Points:** [2–4 bullets on proof, benefits, or details.]  
  - **Call to Action:** [Exact CTA text and intent.]

* `### Visual Direction`  
  [Guidance on imagery, layout, color, typography, and any key visual motifs.]

* `### Tone & Voice Application`  
  [How the defined tone shows up in headlines, body copy, and microcopy, with 2–3 example lines.]

* `### Variations & Adaptations`  
  [List any required variants (sizes, platforms, A/B versions) and how the concept adapts to each.]

* `### Production Notes`  
  [Any execution details: animation ideas, transitions, photography needs, accessibility considerations, or handoff notes.]

## Quality Controls
* Ensure the creative concept directly supports the primary objective.
* Confirm the asset aligns with brand guidelines and target audience needs.
* Maintain clear, concise messaging with a single dominant key message.
* Check that all required elements (logos, CTAs, disclaimers) are included.
* Optimize for the specific platform and context of use.
